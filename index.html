Ashley R. Houston, PhD

Researcher | Social Epidemiologist | Medical Anthropologist

Currently, I work as a Strategic Researcher for the Boston Teachers Union. I remain affiliated faculty at the Institute of Health Equity and Social Justice Research at Northeastern University.

In my research, I focus on identifying root causes of health inequities to aid in their prevention. I examine how social and economic inequalities influence distributions of health and access to healthcare in addition to how communities resist oppress as unique forms of public health intervention. In my dissertation, I examine the ways migration and structural oppression are interwoven in the U.S., through policy, employment practices, health care, and in housing. Methodologically, I draw on my interdisciplinary background to utilize diverse tools from qualitative, ethnographic methods to quanitative, epidemiological methods and mixed methods.

Outside of teaching and research, you can find me reading, crafting, catching live music, or organizing for a brighter future.
