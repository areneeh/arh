Somali Youth Longitudinal Study

Since 2018, I have worked with longitudinal, mixed methods data from the Somali Youth Longitudinal Study (SYLS) funded by the National Institutes of Minority Health and Health Disparities and National Institute of Justice in collaboration between Boston Children's Hospital and Northeastern University. My research using SYLS data primarily focuses on: (1) structural determinants of Somali young adults healthcare utilization and (2) intersectional discrimination, social belonging, and mental health.

Sanctuary Cities and Immigrant Health

At Northeastern University, I collaborated on the Sanctuary Cities project funded by the Global Resiliance Institute at Northeastern University. This qualitative project assesses how organizational workers in two U.S. sanctuary cities: Boston, MA and Seattle, WA, conceptualize "sanctuary" and understand immigrant safety, health, and wellbeing in these spaces. My research using this data focuses on: (1) how organizational workers develop sanctuary practices to mitigate limitations of sanctuary policy and (2) how popular messages of sanctuary and safety may conflict with organizational workers' definitions of safety.

Immigrant and Health Policy in Massachusetts

At Northeastern University, I collaborated with Dr. Tiffany Joseph from the Department of Sociology and Anthropology to analyze longitudinal qualitative data among organizational workers and healthcare providers in Boston, Massachusetts. In this project, Dr. Joseph examines how documentation status and race influence immigrants' healthcare utilization and experiences over time, as local and state policies change.
