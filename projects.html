Somali Youth Longitudinal Study

As a doctoral student and affiliated faculty at the Institute for Health Equity and Social Justice Research at Northeastern University, I have assessed data from the Somali Youth Longitudinal Study (SYLS). My research using the SYLS data primarily focuses on: (1) structural determinants of Somali young adults healthcare utilization and (2) intersectional discrimination, social belonging, and mental health.

Sanctuary Cities and Immigrant Health

As a doctoral student at Northeastern University, I collaborated on the Sanctuary Cities project. This qualitative study assesses how organizational workers in two U.S. sanctuary cities: Boston, MA and Seattle, WA, conceptualize "sanctuary" and understand immigrant health and wellbeing in these spaces. My work on this project focuses on: (1) how organizational workers develop sanctuary practices to mitigate limitations of sanctuary policy and (2) how popular messages of sanctuary and safety may conflict with organizational workers' definitions of "safety".

Immigrant and Health Policy in Massachusetts

As a doctoral student, I collaborated with Dr. Tiffany Joseph to analyze longitudinal qualitative data among organizational workers and healthcare providers in Boston, Massachusetts. In this project, Dr. Joseph examines how documentation status and race influence immigrants' healthcare utilization and experiences over time, as local and state policies change.
